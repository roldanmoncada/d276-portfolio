Career Goals

My primary goal is to complete this program within five years.

Secondarily, I want to become a software developer at an enterprise-level company.

My long-term goal is to become experienced and skilled enough to be able to create my own business.

None of these goals can become reality without first completing this bachelors-to-masters program. Prior to becoming a professional, I aim to take an internship position during or shortly after the final year of the bachelor's portion. My partner graduates from medical school in May of 2027, so I do not believe I will be in Salt Lake City at that point. This is to take place wherever we end up. After this internship and during the beginning of the master's portion of the program, my plan is to either be hired at the company in which I interned, or elsewhere. Ideally the location I am first employed at will be where I acquire my first four or five years of experience as a devloper.

At this benchmark, I will evaluate my situation and consider the possibility of creating my own business. If my experience is not enough for such an undertaking or if my company is providing an acceptable amount of upward mobility, I will either stay at the same company or move to another to challenge myself and develop further, professionally. During this chunk of time I also want to be bolstering my personal portfolio, doing freelance work for extra hands-on experience, and perhaps even being a leader in my company or community.

Once I am finally qualified to create and run my own business, I will do so on my own or with a business partner or two. As years go by, I aim to scale locally before then expanding my scope to offer my services to nearby states. The long-, long-term goal is to be able to hire out management of the company so I can come and go as I please so that I can pursue philanthropic efforts. All of that, though, cannot be achieved without first paying my dues during my tenure at WGU.
